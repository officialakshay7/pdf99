What does splitting a PDF actually do?

Splitting extracts pages from a PDF into separate files. The source document is not modified — splitting creates a new file containing only the pages you selected. Common uses include: sharing a few pages from a confidential report, separating a multi-invoice scan into individual invoice files, extracting a single chapter from a long document, or removing unwanted pages from a scanned document.

PDF99's split tool goes further than most by rendering each page as a visible thumbnail, so you can confirm you're selecting the right pages before extracting.
