It means taking a larger PDF - maybe 5MB or more - and squeezing it down to roughly 100 kilobytes while keeping it readable. Best practices for hitting 100KB

  1. Start with “Extreme” if size is critical. It guarantees under 100KB, though quality may dip slightly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it really 100% private?

Yes. Your files are processed in your browser - we never see them, and they never leave your computer.

What if my PDF is already under 100KB?

Then you probably don't need compression! But you can still use the tool - it won't enlarge the file.

Can I get exactly 100KB every time?

The final size depends on content. Our algorithm aims for ≤100KB in Extreme mode, but slight variations may occur. The sliders let you dial in closer.

Will scanned documents become unreadable?

Not at all. Even at 100KB, scanned text remains legible. You can preview the result by toggling the quality settings.

Can I compress multiple PDFs at once?

Absolutely. Select several files and we'll compress each to ~100KB. Download individually or as a ZIP.

Is there a file size limit?

Only your device's memory. Most modern computers handle PDFs up to 100MB without breaking a sweat.

How do the sliders affect file size?

Lower JPEG quality (10-20%) and render scale (0.8-1.0) produce smaller files. Increase them for better clarity at the cost of size.

Do I need to create an account?

No sign-up, no payments - it's completely free and anonymous.

We can't open encrypted files. Remove the password first, then compress.

Will text be searchable after compression?

The text becomes part of the image, so you can't select it as text, but you can search the PDF if it was born-digital. For scanned documents, it's image-only.
